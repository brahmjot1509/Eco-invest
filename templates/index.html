ESG analysis integrates these factors to assess the overall sustainability and ethical performance of companies or investment opportunities. Investors use this analysis to make more informed decisions that align with their values and long-term objectives.

(E) Environmental Criteria

Environmental criteria are concerned with the environmental stewardship of a corporation. The environmental component of ESG investigates how a firm uses natural resources and affects the environment. Climate change is the primary concern of environmental elements.

(S) Social Criteria

Social criteria are concerned with a company's social obligation. The social component of ESG investigates how a firm handles its connections with its staff and other community stakeholders. The social ramifications of business choices are the primary concern of social considerations.

(G) Governance Criteria

The integrity of a company's governance system is the focus of governance criteria. ESG governance investigates whether a company's leadership operates within an ethical and responsible framework. The primary focus of governance considerations is leadership accountability.
